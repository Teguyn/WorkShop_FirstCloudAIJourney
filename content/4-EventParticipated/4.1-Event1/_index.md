# Summary Report: Event on May 9th

### Event Objectives

- Share "brain hack" techniques to build effective study and work habits, avoiding the temptation of social media.
- Guide on project architecture design and cost optimization on Cloud/AI.
- Prepare the mindset and essential skills for students/freshers in the AI era (AI Ready Freshers).
- Introduce modern software development methods (Bmad) and Context Engineering techniques when working with AI.

### Speakers

- **Mr. Hoang Long** - Topic: Studying like social media addiction
- **Mr. Thinh** - Topic: Automated prompt generation for AI
- **Mr. Khang** - Topic: Why not working yet - AI ready freshers
- **Ms. Thao** - Topic: AI Technologies and the Bmad Framework

---

### Key Highlights

#### Mr. Hoang Long: Studying like social media addiction
- **Brain Decoding**: The brain often prioritizes social media over studying because social media provides fast rewards (dopamine), triggers curiosity, and consumes very little energy. On the contrary, studying requires long concentration, delayed outcomes, and no immediate rewards.
- **Turning the Brain into a "Dopamine Game"**: The key to motivation is recognizing the value of the reward for each study session. A clear reward/penalty mechanism must be established (e.g., creating a lucky draw box after studying) to build reward loops.
- **3 "Brain Hack" Tips**:
    - **Using Fear to Learn**: Humans tend to fear loss (fear of missing out/losing) more than enjoying gains. Create a "study streak" to generate a fear of breaking the streak.
    - **Chunking Study Time**: Tricking the brain's nucleus: break study sessions into 10-minute blocks, repeating multiple times rather than studying continuously for hours.
    - **Creating Feedback Loops**: After completing a task, reward yourself with a 10-minute break. Apply social media mechanisms to learning, such as streaks, leaderboards, and anticipation for the next event.
- **Conclusion**: Winners are not those who are always motivated, but those who know how to turn motivation into a habit.

#### Mr. Thinh: Automated prompt generation for AI
- Presented completely in English with a professional international style.
- Detailed the project architecture and how to connect various services.
- Analyzed the causes and designed structures that keep operational costs very low.
- Deep dived into the structure and methods for generating effective automated prompts for AI systems.

#### Mr. Khang: Why not working yet - Mindset for "AI Ready Freshers"
- **Overcoming AI Fear**: The AI boom makes students feel overwhelmed and afraid of being replaced. The solution is to focus on unchanging core values and master 5 foundational services to start.
- **Principles of Using AI**: Use AI but thoroughly understand the essence. There is no absolute right or wrong, only better implementation. Always question the results generated by AI. Do not hesitate to ask the community for support, and dare to make mistakes to learn.
- **Integrity**: Crucial for teamwork. Show everyone that you are someone who "follows through to the end," thereby building solid trust with teammates.
- **3 Work Circles**: What you love - What brings benefits - What you have to do.
- **5 Benefits of Working**: Salary, Experience, Network, Knowledge/Skills, Personal Growth.
- **Corporate Evaluation Criteria**: Attitude, Qualifications, Experience, Life experience, and Qualities.

#### Ms. Thao: Software Development Methods & Bmad Framework
- **Warning on "Vibe Coding"**: Coding with AI by feeling (Vibe coding) is only suitable for demos and cannot create production-ready software. Overuse causes developers to forget core concepts.
- **The Shift**: Shift thinking from simple "Prompting" to "Context Engineering" to provide enough data for AI to comprehend the entire system context.
- **Introducing Bmad (on GitHub)**: A framework that perfectly fits current software development standards.
- **Standard AI Workflow**:
    - Assign roles to AI, defining which AI member handles what.
    - Create a closed pipeline: Brainstorm -> Project Init -> Coder (when enough scenarios/data exist) -> Tester -> Iterate.
    - Critical note: Use separate chat sessions for each task/role. Do not combine everything in one chat to prevent context confusion.

### Key Takeaways

#### Mindset & Soft Skills
- Understood the brain's mechanism regarding procrastination and how to apply "Dopamine" to gamify studying into a daily habit.
- Deeply recognized the importance of "Integrity" in teamwork – committing to follow through to build trust.
- Shaped the mindset of a Fresher in the AI era: Do not panic before vast knowledge, focus on core foundations (5 key services), and always maintain a healthy skepticism to verify AI outputs.

#### Architecture & AI Technical Concepts
- Understood the essence of Context Engineering in interacting with LLMs to build optimal, cost-effective prompt sequences.
- Eliminated unstructured "Vibe coding" habits, adopting the Bmad method to construct production-ready projects.
- Mastered AI role-playing configurations: setting up separate chats for Brainstorm, Coder, and Tester to create a closed, consistent pipeline.

#### Applying to Work & Current Projects
- **Personal Time Management**: Applied chunked study time (10 mins/block) and streaks to maintain progress on AWS Labs and AI Riddle Generator tasks without losing momentum.
- **Interacting with Amazon Bedrock**: In the internship project, shifted from basic prompting to Context Engineering. By defining roles (e.g., educational expert) and system context, AI returns accurate JSON for riddle generation.
- **Code Management**: Never use a single AI chat stream to code the entire project. Split work into separate sessions for Frontend (React), Backend (Lambda), and Database (DynamoDB) following Ms. Thao's advice to prevent loop errors.
