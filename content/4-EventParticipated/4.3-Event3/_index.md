# Summary Report: Event on June 20th

### Event Objectives

- Create an academic playground to help interns review, test, and consolidate in-depth knowledge of Amazon Web Services (AWS) services.
- Train teamwork, critical thinking, and strategic decision-making skills under time pressure.
- Connect the intern community, interact, and learn directly from seniors through solving difficult case studies.

### Coordinating Team

- **Mentors from FCAJ**: Acted as judges and program coordinators. They not only heated up the event atmosphere but also directly explained difficult questions in detail, helping contestants understand the technical essence instead of just memorizing answers.

### Key Highlights: Competition Progress
- The event took place in the form of a direct match between teams, using multiple-choice questions and AWS scenarios. The tactical highlight of the competition was the right to use the **"Lucky Star"**, which brought an opportunity to turn the tables but also came with high risk.

#### Qualification Round (4 Highly Dramatic Matches)

- **Match 1 (Question Set 3): primeOps vs KLKAT**
    - The two teams chased each other closely through 10 questions. Team **KLKAT** made a spectacular comeback when they decided to use the "Lucky Star" on the final question worth 50 points to win convincingly.

- **Match 2 (Question Set 1): YoungflameIt vs Teamnewbies**
    - The scenario of Match 1 repeated as **YoungflameIt** showed excellent risk calculation. They used the "Lucky Star" on the final decisive question, successfully coming back to win a ticket to the next round.

- **Match 3 (Question Set 2): Loser vs Ngu dai hiep**
    - Team **Ngu dai hiep** performed with ultimate calm and confidence. They gathered points question by question and won overwhelmingly without using the lucky star. On the contrary, team **Loser**, true to its name, bet everything on the lucky star on the final question but answered incorrectly, resulting in being left far behind in points.

- **Match 4 (Question Set 5): Gap phai thang lieu vs Life long learner**
    - This was the most intense match of the qualifying round, with both teams tied with perfect scores up to question 10. The organizers were forced to use question 10 of set 4 as a tie-breaker. With quick intelligence and responsiveness, **Life long learner** won the final victory.

#### Semifinal Round (Finding the Champion)

- **Semifinal 1 (Question Set 7): KLKAT vs YoungflameIt**
    - The two "comeback kings" of the qualifying round faced each other, creating a breathtaking match. The turning point occurred on question 9 when **KLKAT** decided to release the "Lucky Star" early, answered correctly, and created a safe score gap. Pushed to the wall, **YoungflameIt** was forced to use the star on question 10 to find a way out, but unfortunately answered incorrectly. **KLKAT** proudly advanced to the finals.

- **Semifinal 2 (Question Set 6): Ngu dai hiep vs Life long learner**
    - Team **Life long learner** started very well and took the lead temporarily. However, as the subsequent questions increased in difficulty, the calm and solid knowledge base of **Ngu dai hiep** paid off. They gradually equalized the score, then rose strongly to win, officially entering the final match.
    *(Note: The Final Match between KLKAT and Ngu dai hiep will be rescheduled to another event).*

### Key Takeaways

#### Professional Knowledge (AWS)

- Through detailed explanations from FCAJ mentors after each difficult question, I patched many gaps in my knowledge of how AWS services work together, especially real-world case studies requiring cost and performance optimization comparison.
- Acquired more tips and tricks to handle complex scenario-based questions in AWS certification exams.

#### Strategic Thinking & Soft Skills

- **Risk Management**: The lesson from using the "Lucky Star" showed the thin line between success and failure. Deciding when to go "all-in" (like KLKAT, YoungflameIt) and when to play safe and steady (like Ngu dai hiep) is a critical decision-making skill in a real-world working environment.
- **Mental Toughness**: Keeping calm when falling behind or when facing a decisive question that determines the team's victory or defeat.

#### Applying to Work

- **Consolidating the Current Project**: The AWS knowledge reviewed through the competition (especially Compute and Database services) made me much more confident when configuring and optimizing costs for the active "AI Riddle Generator" project.
- **Prudent Mindset**: Just like considering the use of the "Lucky Star," before changing a major structure in the project or deleting an AWS resource, I learned to carefully evaluate the risk ratio (impact) instead of operating based on emotion.

### Event Experience

- The event on June 20th brought an explosive atmosphere, very different from ordinary academic seminars. Learning through Gamification with high competition helped knowledge sink in very naturally.
- Sitting in the audience watching the matches, I felt the suspense with every second of the teams' thinking, especially the moment the results of the "Lucky Star" questions were announced. Seeing how team Ngu dai hiep calmly overcame difficulties with actual knowledge, or how KLKAT seized opportunities precisely, I realized that a strong team needs not only technical expertise but also cool heads and proper strategies. The commentaries and explanations from FCAJ mentors after each round were indeed "priceless" lessons helping us understand the cloud computing world more deeply.
